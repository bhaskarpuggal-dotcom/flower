Flowers

Flowers, also known as blooms and blossoms, are the reproductive structures of flowering plants. Typically, they are structured in four circular levels around the end of a stalk. These include: sepals, which are modified leaves that support the flower; petals, often designed to attract pollinators; male parts, where pollen is presented; and female parts, where pollen is received and its movement is facilitated to the egg. When flowers are arranged in a group, they are known collectively as an inflorescence.

The development of flowers is a complex and important part in the life cycles of flowering plants. In most plants, flowers are able to produce sex cells of both sexes. Pollen, which can produce the male sex cells, is transported between the male and female parts of flowers in pollination. Pollination can occur between different plants, as in cross-pollination, or between flowers on the same plant or even the same flower, as in self-pollination. Pollen movement may be caused by animals, such as birds and insects, or non-living things like wind and water. The colour and structure of flowers assist in the pollination process.

List of Flower Types

  1. Rose
  2. Tulip
  3. Sunflower
  4. Lily
  5. Orchid
  6. Daisy
  7. Hibiscus
  8. Lavender
  9. Cherry Blossom (Sakura)
  10. Carnation


  1. Rose – Symbol of love and romance, comes in many colors like red, white, pink, yellow.
  2. Tulip – Spring flower with cup-shaped petals, represents elegance and perfect love.
  3. Sunflower – Bright yellow blooms that follow the sun, symbolizing happiness and loyalty.
  4. Lily – Large fragrant flowers, often white, symbolizing purity, rebirth, and sympathy.
  5. Orchid – Exotic and elegant, symbolizing beauty, strength, and luxury.
  6. Daisy – Simple cheerful flower with white petals and yellow center, symbol of innocence.
  7. Hibiscus – Tropical flower with bright colors, representing delicate beauty and hospitality.
  8. Lavender – Purple fragrant spikes, symbolizing calmness, devotion, and serenity.
  9. Cherry Blossom (Sakura) – Famous in Japan, symbol of renewal and the fleeting beauty of life.
  10. Carnation – Ruffled petals in many colors, symbolizing love, admiration, and gratitude.
